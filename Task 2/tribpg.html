Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She gained international prominence after surviving an assassination attempt by the Taliban in 2012 for her advocacy for girls' education.

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She was born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, Pakistan. Malala became an advocate for education when she was just 11 years old, writing a blog under a pseudonym for the BBC about her life under Taliban rule in Pakistan's Swat Valley. She spoke out against the Taliban's ban on girls' education, and her activism gained international attention.
